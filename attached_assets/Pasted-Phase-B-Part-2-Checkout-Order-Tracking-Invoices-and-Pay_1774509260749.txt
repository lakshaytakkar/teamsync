Phase B Part 2: Checkout, Order Tracking, Invoices, and Payment MilestonesThis prompt completes the ordering system — what happens after the partner fills their cart.Checkout is a single-page flow. The partner reviews their complete order: all items with quantities and prices, the total amount, and their delivery address (pre-filled from their profile, editable). Below the order summary, show the payment section. For now, implement a mock Razorpay button — when they click "Pay ₹[total]," simulate a successful payment after a 2-second loading state, then show a success screen with a confetti moment or a green checkmark and the message "Order placed successfully! Your order number is ORD-0042." Include a "Download Invoice" button and a "View Order Status" button.When a real Razorpay integration comes later, this mock button gets replaced with the actual Razorpay checkout widget — the rest of the flow stays identical. For now, every mock checkout creates an order entry in a local mock JSON state.After checkout, the order needs to appear in the "My Orders" page. This page shows all orders in reverse chronological order (newest first). Each order row shows: order number (ORD-0001 format), order date, number of items, total amount, payment status (a green "Paid" badge or yellow "Pending"), and fulfillment status with a clear visual: "Processing" (orange — we received it, working on it), "Shipped" (blue — it's on its way), "Delivered" (green — partner received it), or "Partially Shipped" (blue-yellow — some items shipped, some still processing, common when order has both express and standard items).Clicking an order opens the order detail page showing: all line items with product name, quantity, price, and line total. The order summary with subtotal, fees, and total. Payment details — when it was paid, the method, a reference number. And a fulfillment timeline — a vertical progress tracker showing the order moving through stages: "Order Placed" → "Payment Confirmed" → "Processing" → "Shipped" → "Delivered" with dates for completed stages and "Estimated" dates for future stages. If the order has both express and standard items, show them in two groups: "Express Items (3-7 days)" and "Standard Items (30-45 days)" with separate status for each group since they ship at different times.Invoices — for every paid order, the partner should be able to view and download a PDF invoice. The invoice should show: our company header (EazyToSell business name, address, GSTIN), the partner's business details (store name, address, GSTIN if they have one), invoice number (matching the order number), invoice date, a table of all items with product name, HSN code, quantity, rate, and amount, subtotal, GST if applicable (we can decide this later — for now just show total), grand total, payment status "PAID" with the payment date, and a footer with terms and conditions. For now this can be a nicely formatted HTML page that they print to PDF, or a simple frontend-generated PDF. Don't overbuild the PDF generation — a clean HTML page styled with @media print CSS is fine for now.Payment Milestones — this is separate from product orders. This tracks the staged payments for the entire store launch journey, not individual product purchases. When a partner signs up, their payment journey has defined milestones:Milestone 0 is the token payment of ₹1 lakh (already paid by the time they're in the portal). Milestone 1 is space confirmation at ₹1.5-2 lakh (triggers when they confirm their store location). Milestone 2 is interior start at ₹2-3 lakh (triggers when the design is approved and construction begins). Milestone 3 is inventory order at 50% of inventory value (triggers when the first product order is placed). Milestone 4 is goods ready at the remaining 50% (triggers when products arrive at our warehouse and are ready to ship). After that it's replenishment — ongoing per-order payments which are tracked through the regular orders system, not milestones.The Milestones page shows a vertical timeline of these 5 milestones. Each milestone shows: milestone name, amount due, status (paid with green check, due with yellow clock, upcoming with gray circle, overdue with red alert), the date it was paid or the date it's due, and a "Pay Now" button for milestones that are currently due. The "Pay Now" button would eventually trigger Razorpay, but for now it shows a mock payment flow same as the order checkout.Load all order data from mockOrders.json — create 4-5 sample orders in different states (one delivered, one shipped, one processing, one with mixed shipment). Load milestone data from mockMilestones.json — show milestones 0 and 1 as paid, milestone 2 as due, and 3-4 as upcoming.Everything on these pages should connect to what the POS system expects. When we defined the POS inventory system, we said stock enters the store through "Stock Receive" on the POS side. So here's how the full circle works: partner places an order here in Phase B → our fulfillment team processes it (separately tracked on our side) → they ship it → partner marks it delivered here → then on the POS side (Phase C) the store staff does a Stock Receive to scan everything into inventory. The order tracking here and the stock receive in POS are two sides of the same delivery. They don't need to be technically linked in this prompt (mock data), but the UX should make it obvious: when an order status changes to "Delivered," the partner should see a nudge like "Your order has arrived! Go to Store Operations → Stock Receive to add these products to your inventory."
