The Partner Dashboard, Profile, Support, and Everything Tying Together
This is the glue prompt that makes everything feel like one coherent portal instead of three separate apps bolted together.
The Partner Dashboard is the first thing the partner sees when they log in. It should be smart enough to show different things based on where they are in their journey.
If the store status is "setup" (store not yet active), the dashboard should feel like a launch countdown. Show: a progress banner at the top ("Your store is 65% ready — 8 tasks remaining"), their onboarding completion percentage with a "Continue Setup" button if not complete, a summary of their BOQ status from Phase A ("18 of 22 setup items selected, estimated cost ₹2.1L"), their order status from Phase B ("2 orders placed — 1 delivered, 1 in transit"), their milestone payment status ("3 of 5 milestones paid, ₹1.5L due for Interior Start"), and the readiness checklist summary ("12 of 44 items complete — next: install shelving"). Each of these cards links to the relevant page deeper in the portal. The vibe should be: here's everything you need to do to open your store, and here's how far along you are.
If the store status is "active" (store is open and operating), the dashboard transforms into a daily business summary. Show: today's sales total and transaction count from POS data (this connects directly to what POS Prompt 1 creates), stock health from inventory (how many products are healthy vs low vs out of stock — from POS Prompt 2), cash register status (whether the register is currently open, and if so, the running cash total — from POS Prompt 3), recent orders from Phase B (any pending deliveries or recent arrivals), and a low stock alert card showing the top 5 items that need reordering soon (from POS Prompt 2's low stock alerts). Each card links to the relevant page. The vibe should be: here's how your business is doing right now, and here's what needs your attention.
For mock data, create a mockDashboard.json with two variants — one for "setup" status and one for "active" status — so the developer can toggle between them using the store status mock.
The Profile page should show and allow editing of: the partner's personal info (name, phone, email), their store details (store name, address, city, state, area in sq ft, GSTIN, PAN), their selected package (Lite/Pro/Elite — read-only, set by our team), and their assigned relationship manager from our team (name and WhatsApp contact — read-only). This overlaps with some data from onboarding and from POS Prompt 3's store settings, so make sure they're reading from the same mock data source. If the partner updates their store name here, it should reflect on the POS receipt header. Same data, one source of truth.
The Support page should have: a FAQ section with 10-15 common questions and answers loaded from mockFAQs.json (things like "How do I scan products?", "How do I process a return?", "When will my order arrive?", "How do I add a cashier?", "What if my barcode scanner stops working?"), a "Contact Your Manager" card with the assigned manager's name, phone, and a WhatsApp deep link (wa.me/91XXXXXXXXXX with a pre-filled message "Hi, I need help with my store [store name]"), a "Call Support" button with the support phone number, and an "Email Support" button with a mailto link. Keep it simple — no ticket system, no chat widget. Partners in Tier 2/3 India communicate primarily through WhatsApp and phone calls. Meet them where they are.
The Onboarding flow should either be left as it currently exists (it's already built with a 5-step wizard) or be reorganized to flow naturally into Phase A. The existing onboarding collects: personal details, store identity, store dimensions, inventory preferences (category mix sliders), and a review step. After completing onboarding, the partner should land in the Phase A Store Setup section as their natural next step. The flow should feel like: "Great, you've told us about your store. Now let's plan what you need to build it."
One final connection point: the staff/cashier management we defined in Prompt 1 of this series. The partner's settings page (which overlaps with POS Prompt 3's store settings) should include the Staff section where they manage cashiers. When a cashier logs in, they should skip the dashboard entirely and land directly on the POS billing screen — that's their universe. The navigation for a cashier should be minimal: Billing (default landing), Stock Receive, and Logout. Nothing else. No dashboard, no orders, no settings, no reports. If a cashier tries to access any other URL, redirect them back to billing.
To summarize how all the Partner Portal prompts connect to the POS prompts we already defined:
The partner navigates through Phase A (store building — Prompt 2 of this series), then Phase B (catalog and orders — Prompts 3 and 4 of this series), and when the store goes live, Phase C unlocks and becomes their primary workspace (POS Prompts 1, 2, and 3 that we defined earlier). The dashboard (Prompt 5 of this series) adapts to show relevant info from whichever phase is active. Staff management (Prompt 1 of this series) creates the cashier sub-role that the POS billing screen uses. Product orders in Phase B lead to stock receives in Phase C — the partner orders from our catalog, receives the delivery, then scans everything into their POS inventory. And the data from Phase C (daily sales, stock levels, popular products) eventually feeds back into Phase B to power smart reorder suggestions.
All of it: one portal, one login, progressive unlocking, mock JSON data that swaps to real APIs later.
