Tickets, Notifications, and Team Coordination
The Tickets page is a simple support ticket system. When a partner has a problem or a question that can't be resolved in a quick WhatsApp chat, the ops person creates a ticket. A ticket has: a title describing the issue, the client it's linked to, a priority (Low, Medium, High, Urgent), a status (Open, In Progress, Waiting on Partner, Waiting on Team, Resolved), who it's assigned to (any team member — could be ops, could be Bharti for a design issue, could be Khushal for a warehouse issue), a description of the problem, and a conversation thread where team members can add updates. When the ticket is resolved, the ops person changes status to Resolved and adds a closing note.
The Tickets page shows all tickets in a filterable table: ticket number, title, client name, priority (color-coded — red for urgent, orange for high, yellow for medium, gray for low), status, assigned to, days open, and last updated. Filter by: status (open vs resolved), priority, assigned to me vs all tickets, and client. There should be a quick count bar at the top: 3 urgent, 5 high, 12 medium, 4 low — all clickable to filter.
The ops dashboard should also have a "Batch View" — since we launch stores in batches of 3-5, the ops person needs to see all stores in the current batch at a glance. A batch card shows: batch name ("Batch 1 — March 2026"), the 3-5 clients in this batch, each one's current stage, overall batch progress (e.g., "2 of 3 stores have completed interior, 1 pending"), and any blockers or overdue items across the batch. This helps the ops person coordinate parallel launches.
There should be a Team Assignments view showing who on the team is responsible for what across all active clients. A simple matrix: team members as rows, clients as columns, and what they're doing in each cell. For example: Bharti has "Design phase" for Client A and "Signage pending" for Client B. Khushal has "Inventory at warehouse" for Client A and "Waiting for shipment" for Client C. This prevents things from falling through cracks. Load from mockTickets.json and mockBatches.json.
