Inventory Management, Stock Receive, Stock Adjustments, and Alerts
Now that billing works and every sale automatically deducts stock, we need the full inventory picture — knowing exactly what's in the store, adding new stock when deliveries arrive, fixing mismatches when physical counts don't match the system, and catching low stock before it becomes a problem.
The Inventory page is the store owner's stock control center — inspired by Marg's stock report but much simpler since we don't have batches, expiry dates, or multiple warehouses. It shows every product in the store as a searchable, filterable table. Each row shows: product name, barcode, category, MRP, current stock quantity, a color-coded status badge (green for healthy stock above the reorder threshold, yellow for low stock at or below threshold but not zero, red for out of stock at zero), and the value of that stock (quantity times cost price, visible only if the logged-in user is the store owner, not regular staff). At the top of the page, four summary cards: total SKUs tracked, healthy count, low stock count, and out of stock count. Also show total inventory value (sum of all stock times cost price — again, owner-only visibility). The table should be filterable by category and by status (show me only red items, or only yellow). Search should work on product name and barcode.
Clicking any product row should open a detail panel showing that product's stock movement history — a timeline of every increase and decrease: "Sale R-0042: -2 units" on March 25, "Stock Receive SR-003: +24 units" on March 23, "Manual Adjustment: +3 units (reason: found in backroom)" on March 20. This audit trail is how the store owner investigates discrepancies. Every movement shows: date, type (sale, receive, return, adjustment), quantity change (positive or negative), the reference (receipt number or receive batch), and who did it.
Stock Receive is what the staff uses when new inventory arrives at the store. This follows Marg's "Purchase Challan" pattern but massively simplified — no supplier name, no purchase price, no GST. The store is receiving products from US (EazyToSell), and the portal already tracked the order on our side. The store staff just needs to confirm what physically arrived.
The flow: staff opens Stock Receive page, the system auto-creates a new receive session with a reference number (like SR-001, SR-002 incrementing). They scan each incoming product's barcode (same auto-focus input pattern as billing), enter the quantity received, and it adds a line to the receive list. The list shows: product name, barcode, quantity being received. They keep scanning until everything is counted. They can also search by product name as fallback. When done, they tap "Confirm Receive" — all quantities get added to the store's inventory. Each item creates a stock movement record with type "receive" and the SR reference number. If a product doesn't exist in the store's inventory yet (first time receiving it), the system creates a new inventory record for that product at that store.
There should also be a receive history page showing all past stock receives — date, reference number, total items received, who received it. Clicking one shows the full list of items received in that session. This is important for us too — if a partner says "I never got those 24 kitchen organizers," we can check the receive log.
Stock Adjustment is inspired by Marg's "Physical Stock" feature. Sometimes the system says there are 12 units but the shelf has 15 — or worse, the system says 10 but there are only 6. The store owner needs to fix this. The flow: they go to Stock Adjustment, search for or scan a product, see the system's current count, enter the actual physical count, and the system calculates the difference. They must enter a reason — dropdown with options like "physical count correction," "damaged and removed," "found in backroom," "lost or stolen," "miscounted during receive." When they confirm, the system adjusts inventory to match reality and logs a stock movement with type "adjustment," the quantity difference, and the reason. This is an owner-only action — regular cashier staff should not be able to adjust stock, only the store owner.
Low Stock Alerts should be a dedicated page showing all products where current stock has fallen below the reorder threshold (which defaults to 5 units but is configurable per store in settings). Sorted by urgency: out of stock at the top in red, then critically low (1-2 units) in dark yellow, then low (3-5 units or whatever the threshold is) in light yellow. For each product show: name, current stock, average daily sales rate (calculated from the last 14 days of POS data — total units sold divided by 14), estimated days until stockout (current stock divided by daily rate, or "OUT" if already zero), and a suggested reorder quantity (daily rate times 30 for a month's supply, rounded up). This page is the bridge to the smart reorder system we'll build later — for now it's just information, not action. But it tells the store owner exactly what's running low and how fast.
The database needs: an inventory table (store_id, product_id, current_stock as integer, reorder_threshold defaulting to 5, cost_price for valuation, last_updated timestamp — unique constraint on store_id plus product_id so each product has one row per store), a stock_movements table (store_id, product_id, movement_type as enum of sale/receive/return/adjustment, quantity_change as integer which is negative for sales and positive for receives, reference_type and reference_id to link back to what caused it, reason for adjustments, performed_by user reference, timestamp), and a stock_receives table (store_id, reference_number auto-incrementing per store, total_items counted, received_by user, timestamp, with individual line items stored either as separate rows in a stock_receive_items table or as JSON — developer's choice).
