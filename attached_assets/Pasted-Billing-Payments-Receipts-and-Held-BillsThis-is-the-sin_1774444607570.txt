Billing, Payments, Receipts, and Held BillsThis is the single most important screen in the entire portal. It's where every rupee of revenue enters the system. A store cashier will use this screen 50-200 times a day, so it needs to be the fastest, cleanest page we build. Think of Marg's "Counter Sale" mode — no party name needed, no credit terms, no GST breakdown visible to the cashier. Just scan, quantity, pay, print, next customer.This whole POS section only appears when the partner's store status is "active" in our system. Until our ops team flips that switch, these pages show a locked screen with a message like "Complete your store setup to unlock daily operations."The billing page needs a full-screen two-panel layout — no topbar navigation eating up space. The left panel is the transaction workspace, the right panel is for quick actions and payment.Left panel, top to bottom: First, a barcode input field that is always auto-focused when the page loads. This is how 90% of items get added. A USB barcode scanner gun is just a keyboard — it types the barcode number into the focused field and presses Enter. When Enter is pressed, the system looks up that barcode in our product database. If found, the item gets added to the bill with quantity 1. If not found, show an inline red flash message "Not found" and clear the field — no popup, no modal, no interruption. The cashier's flow cannot break. Next to the barcode field, a camera scan button for when the gun breaks — this opens the device camera and reads the barcode optically. Below the barcode input, a search field where the cashier can type a product name. As they type, matching products appear in a dropdown — they click one and it gets added to the bill. This is the fallback for when scanning doesn't work or when a product has no barcode sticker.Below the search is the item grid — this is the current bill, inspired by Marg's bill item grid but much simpler. Each row shows: a row number, the product name, the MRP (selling price), quantity (defaults to 1), and line total (MRP times quantity). The cashier should be able to tap the quantity to change it — either typing a new number or using plus/minus buttons. There should be a delete button on each row to remove an item. As items are added, the grid scrolls and the running total updates instantly at the bottom. The bottom of the left panel shows the item count ("5 items") and the grand total in a large, impossible-to-miss font — this is what the cashier reads aloud to the customer.Right panel: The top section is a grid of "quick add" tiles — 8-12 products that the store sells most often, shown as tappable cards with the product name and price. Think of loose items, items with damaged barcodes, or fast movers that the cashier wants to add with one tap instead of scanning. The store owner should be able to configure which products appear here from the settings. Below the quick-add grid, show a "held bills" indicator — a small card that says "2 bills on hold" (or whatever the count is) with a "Recall" button.At the bottom of the entire screen, a large prominent charge button showing "Charge ₹[total]". Tapping this opens a payment screen — not a new page, just a modal or slide-up panel. Three payment options as big clear buttons: Cash, UPI, and Card.For Cash: show a "Received" field where the cashier enters what the customer handed over. Below it, auto-calculate and display the change to return. A common shortcut: show quick-amount buttons for ₹500, ₹1000, ₹2000 so the cashier can tap instead of typing. If they tap "Exact" it fills in the exact total.For UPI: just one confirmation button "UPI Payment Received." The customer pays using their own phone's GPay/PhonePe to the store's QR code — we're not integrating any UPI gateway at the store level. The cashier just confirms it was received.For Card: same thing — "Card Payment Received." The store has their own card swipe machine and the cashier confirms payment was taken.After confirming any payment method, the sale is instantly complete. The system should immediately show a receipt preview formatted for a 58mm thermal printer — this is the standard receipt printer every Indian retail store uses. The receipt layout: store name centered in large text at top, store address and GSTIN on the next line, a dashed separator, receipt number (auto-generated like "R-0001" incrementing per store) and date/time on one line, another separator, then each item listed with quantity, MRP, and line total, another separator, grand total in large text, payment method, and if cash — the amount received and change returned. At the bottom: "Thank you! Visit again" and "Powered by EazyToSell." The receipt should be about 32 characters wide to fit the 58mm paper. Printing uses the browser's window.print() with print-specific CSS that formats everything to that narrow width. After printing (or dismissing the preview), the screen resets completely — cleared bill, barcode field re-focused, ready for the next customer.Held bills — this is a critical workflow from Marg that many POS systems miss. Customer A is mid-transaction, realizes they forgot something, steps away. Customer B is waiting. The cashier taps "Hold" — the current bill gets saved temporarily with all its items and the screen clears for a new transaction. A badge updates showing "1 bill on hold." When customer A comes back, the cashier taps "Recall," sees a list of held bills (showing item count and partial total for each), picks the right one, and continues the transaction. Held bills expire after 24 hours if not recalled. There should be no limit on how many bills can be held at once, but practically it'll be 1-3.Every completed sale needs to do these things automatically in the background: create a sale record in the database with all line item details, deduct the sold quantities from the store's inventory (if an item has zero stock, still allow the sale — the product is physically there — but log it as a stock discrepancy), and create a stock movement record for each item showing it was reduced due to a sale. The receipt number should be unique per store and auto-increment.The database needs: a sales table (store_id, receipt_number, total_amount, payment_method, cashier info, timestamp, and the individual line items with product_id, quantity, unit_price, line_total for each item), and a held_bills table (store_id, items as saved data, created_at, for temporary storage). The inventory table and stock movements table will be created in the next prompt but this prompt should start deducting from inventory if the table exists, and gracefully skip if it doesn't exist yet.One more thing: this billing screen must work on a basic laptop or a cheap Android tablet. Keep it lightweight. No heavy animations, no background processes that slow down the UI. Every barcode scan should result in an item appearing in the grid within 200 milliseconds. This is the one screen where performance matters more than aesthetics.
