Scripts, Proposals, and Communication ToolkitThe Scripts page is the sales person's cheat sheet for every conversation. Organize it by pipeline stage — when I'm making a Discovery Call, what do I say? When they ask about pricing, what's my reply? When they compare us to a franchise, how do I position us?At the top, a stage selector — the sales person picks which stage they're dealing with and sees relevant scripts for that stage. For example:New Inquiry stage scripts: initial WhatsApp message introducing EazyToSell, a follow-up message if no reply after 2 days, a phone call opening script.Discovery Call stage: a call agenda checklist (what questions to ask — budget, timeline, location status, experience, why they're interested), a brief pitch script explaining what we do and what we're not (not a franchise, no royalties, no brand control — we're a store launch and supply partner), and key points to cover.Proposal Sent stage: a WhatsApp message sharing the proposal, a follow-up message after 3 days, a call script for walking them through the investment packages.Negotiation stage: common objection replies. This is the most important section. Seed it with 10-12 real objections and scripted replies:"Why is this not a franchise?" — Because franchise means you pay royalties, follow their rules, use their brand. Here you own your store, your brand, your profits. We just help you set up and supply products at great prices."₹10-15 lakh is expensive" — Let's break it down. ₹2-3 lakh is your store interior which you'd spend anyway. ₹7-10 lakh is inventory that sits on your shelves and sells — it's not a cost, it's your working capital. Plus there are zero ongoing fees."What if products don't sell?" — Our products are priced at ₹29-299 MRP. At these prices, everything moves. Our category mix is based on what sells in stores like MR.DIY which has 400+ stores in India. Plus you can reorder fast-movers anytime."How long does setup take?" — 30-45 days from the day you confirm your space. We handle sourcing, design, and setup in parallel."What support do I get after launch?" — You get our portal with POS billing, inventory tracking, and smart reorder suggestions. For Pro and Elite packages, you also get 30-90 days of direct support from our team."Can I see a running store?" — We're launching our first batch now. We can share the store design, product catalog, and detailed economics so you can see exactly what you're getting."I want to think about it" — Of course. What specific concerns do you have? I can send you a detailed comparison document. Many of our partners had the same hesitation and are now in the setup phase."What about competition from MR.DIY and similar chains?" — MR.DIY stores are 10,000 sq ft in malls with heavy investment. Your store is 500-1000 sq ft in a local market with a fraction of the investment. You're serving a different customer — the neighborhood shopper who doesn't drive to a mall. And your margins are actually higher because our sourcing costs are similar but your overhead is much lower.Each script should have a "Copy" button that copies the text to clipboard with the lead's name auto-filled where appropriate. For WhatsApp scripts, the copy should also offer a "Send via WhatsApp" button that opens wa.me with the lead's number and the message pre-filled.The Proposals page lets the sales person generate a simple proposal for a specific lead. They select a lead, choose a package (Lite/Pro/Elite), and the system generates a one-page proposal showing: the partner's name and city, the selected package name, what's included (list of deliverables for that package), the investment range, the payment milestone schedule, a timeline from sign-up to store opening, and what's explicitly not included (our Scope & Boundaries — the partner handles daily operations, staffing, rent, local compliance etc.). This should be viewable on screen and printable as a clean PDF.
