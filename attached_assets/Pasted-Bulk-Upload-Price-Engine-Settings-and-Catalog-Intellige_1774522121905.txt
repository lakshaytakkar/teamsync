Bulk Upload, Price Engine Settings, and Catalog Intelligence
The bulk upload feature is essential because we have 10,000+ products from Haoduobao and thousands more from Indian suppliers. Adding them one by one would take months.
The Bulk Upload page has a clear flow: first, download a CSV template that shows all the required columns with example data and explanations. The template columns match the product form fields: name, description, category (using the exact category names from our tree), source, barcode, exw_price_yuan (for China), wholesale_price_inr (for India), units_per_carton, carton_length_cm, carton_width_cm, carton_height_cm, compliance_status, label_status, and suggested_mrp (optional — system will calculate if blank). Second, the product team fills in the CSV and uploads it. Third, the system parses the CSV and shows a preview table with: a green row for products that are valid and ready to import, a yellow row for products with warnings (like a missing optional field), and a red row for products with errors (like a missing required field, an invalid category name, or a duplicate barcode). Fourth, they fix any errors and re-upload, or proceed with just the valid rows. Fifth, they click "Import" and the system creates all the product records. Show a progress bar and a summary when done: "Imported 847 products. 23 skipped due to errors. 12 warnings."
The Price Engine Settings page shows all the configurable variables that the price calculation uses: exchange rate (CNY to INR, currently should be about 11.5), sourcing commission percentage (3% for Haoduobao, 5% for Allen), freight cost per CBM (in INR — varies by shipping method and current rates), insurance percentage, customs duty percentage (can be overridden per category), IGST percentage (can be overridden per category), SW surcharge percentage, CHA and port handling percentage, domestic freight percentage (port to warehouse), MRP tagging cost per unit (in INR), our markup percentage (default for opening inventory vs replenishment), and target store margin percentage. Each setting should be editable with immediate preview of how the change affects a sample product. Like: "If you change the exchange rate from 11.5 to 12.0, the landed cost of a ¥1.80 product goes from ₹42 to ₹44 and the partner price goes from ₹78 to ₹82."
There should also be a "Recalculate All" button that recalculates prices across the entire catalog when settings change — with a confirmation ("This will update prices for 10,247 products. Proceed?") and a progress indicator.
The Catalog Intelligence page is a read-only analytics dashboard for the product team: products by category (how many products in each category — are we heavy on Kitchen and light on Stationery?), price distribution (histogram showing how many products fall in each MRP band — how many at ₹49, how many at ₹99, etc.), margin analysis (average margin by source, by category — where are we making the most money?), compliance summary (how many products are still unflagged — these need review), and source comparison (for products available from both China and India sources, show the price difference — is the China product cheaper after landing costs? By how much?). This helps the product team make smart curation decisions: "We have too many Kitchen products at the ₹199 band and not enough at ₹49-99. Let's find more affordable kitchen items from Haoduobao."
