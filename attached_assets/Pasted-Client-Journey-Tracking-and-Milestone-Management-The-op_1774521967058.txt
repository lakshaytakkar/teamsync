Client Journey Tracking and Milestone Management
The ops team takes over from sales after the token is paid. Their job is to shepherd each partner through the entire setup journey — from token paid through to store launch. They coordinate between the partner, Bharti's design team, Khushal's warehouse, and Love Kumar's tech setup. Think of ops as the project manager for each store launch.
When an ops person logs in, their dashboard shows: total active clients (in stages between "Token Paid" and "Launched"), clients with overdue milestones (payments due but not received), clients with overdue tasks (setup steps that should have been done by now), and open support tickets. Below the numbers, a list of all active clients sorted by urgency — the one closest to their launch date at the top, or the one with the most overdue items. Each client card shows: name, city, current stage, days since token was paid, estimated launch date, and a progress bar showing what percentage of their readiness checklist is complete.
The Client Journey page is the ops team's main workspace. When they click a client, they see a detailed timeline view of that partner's entire journey. The top section shows the partner's details: name, city, store address, store area, package, assigned sales person who brought them in, and the ops person managing them.
Below that, a visual stage tracker — a horizontal progress bar showing all post-sales stages: Token Paid → Space Confirmed → Design Phase → Interior Construction → Inventory Ordered → Goods at Warehouse → MRP Tagging → Dispatched → Store Ready → Launched. The current stage is highlighted. The ops person can advance the client to the next stage by clicking it and confirming. When they advance a stage, the system asks for a note ("Design approved by partner on call") and records who made the change and when.
Below the stage tracker, three tabs:
Milestones tab: shows the payment milestone table — same milestones the partner sees (Token, Space Confirmed, Interior Start, Inventory Order, Goods Ready) but with more detail. The ops person sees: milestone name, amount due, date it was due, whether it's paid (and the payment date and method if so), and can mark milestones as paid by entering the payment date and amount received. This is how our system knows the partner has paid — the ops person confirms it here.
Checklist tab: the 44-item readiness checklist across 7 categories (Infrastructure, Fixtures, Inventory, Billing, Staff, Legal, Launch Prep). The ops person can check off items as they're completed. Some items are editable by the partner too (like "GST Registration Done" or "Staff Hired") and some are ops-only (like "Interior Inspection Passed" or "Inventory Dispatched"). Show completion percentage per category and overall.
Activity Log tab: a timeline of everything that happened with this client — stage changes, milestone payments, checklist items completed, notes added by anyone. Each entry has a timestamp and who did it. The ops person can add manual notes here too ("Called Bharti — shelving vendor confirmed for March 20, delivery expected March 25").
The ops person should also be able to see the partner's BOQ from Phase A (what fixtures they chose), their product orders from Phase B (what they ordered from our catalog), and their store status. But they can only VIEW these — they don't edit them.
