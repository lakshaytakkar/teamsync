QC, MRP Tagging, Dispatch, and Warehouse Dashboard
Once goods are received at the warehouse, three things happen before they can be shipped to the partner: quality check, MRP sticker application, and packaging for dispatch.
The QC page shows all items that have been received and need quality checking. For each batch of received goods, the fulfillment person does a spot check — they don't check every single unit (that would take forever for thousands of items), they check 5-10% randomly per product. The QC flow: select a received vendor order, see the list of products with quantities, and for each product enter: units checked, units passed, units failed, and a note if any failures (e.g., "3 units scratched packaging, 1 unit wrong color"). If the fail rate is above 10% for any product, flag it in red so the team escalates it. Once QC is done for a vendor order, mark it as "QC Passed" or "QC Failed" (if overall failure is too high). Passed items move to MRP Tagging.
The MRP Tagging Queue shows all QC-passed items that need stickers. This is the physical process we defined earlier — the system generates label data (product name, MRP, barcode, our company details as importer, country of origin), the warehouse team prints the stickers on a thermal transfer printer, and applies them to each product. In the portal, this page shows: product name, quantity to tag, MRP to print on the sticker, barcode number, and a status (Pending, In Progress, Done). The fulfillment person updates each product as tagging is completed. They can also print a "tag sheet" — a page listing all the label data for a batch so the person at the printing station knows what to print. Once all items in an order are tagged, the order advances to "Ready to Ship."
The Dispatch page shows all orders that are ready to ship. For each order: partner name, city, delivery address, number of items, total weight (if we track it), and a "Ship" button. When they mark an order as shipped, they enter: shipping provider name (a local trucking partner or courier), tracking number if available, expected delivery date, and the number of boxes. This updates the partner's order status in their portal to "Shipped" and eventually they'll get a notification. When the partner confirms receipt (either in their portal or the ops team confirms it), the order status moves to "Delivered."
The Fulfillment Dashboard ties it all together — a birds-eye view of the entire pipeline. Show: orders by status (a horizontal bar or funnel: 5 New, 3 Split, 4 Ordered, 2 Received, 3 QC, 2 Tagging, 1 Ready, 2 Shipped), average processing time per stage (how long does QC take on average, how long does tagging take — helps spot bottlenecks), overdue items (anything sitting in a stage for too long without progress), and a China batch summary (any active batches, estimated arrival dates, total items in transit). The warehouse team opens this dashboard first thing every morning to plan their day.
