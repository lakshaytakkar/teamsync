Product Catalog CRUD, Compliance, and Categories
The product team manages our entire catalog — what we sell, what it costs, whether it's legal to sell, and how it's organized. When someone on the product team logs in, their portal is essentially a product database management tool.
The Product Dashboard shows catalog health at a glance: total products in the system, breakdown by source (how many from Haoduobao, how many from Allen, how many from each Indian supplier), compliance breakdown (how many safe, restricted, banned — the banned number should ideally be zero because those products shouldn't be in the catalog at all, but if they exist they need to be flagged and removed), products missing key data (no MRP assigned, no barcode, no category, no compliance flag — these are data quality issues), and a source split showing average margin by source (China products average X% margin, India products average Y%).
The Product List is the main working page — a big table showing every product. Columns: product name, category, source, EXW price (in Yuan for China products, in INR for India products), landed cost (calculated), partner price (what we charge the partner), suggested MRP, margin percentage, compliance status (green/yellow/red badge), label status (English/Chinese/Needs Relabel), barcode, and an edit button. The table should be searchable (by name, barcode, or category), sortable by any column, and filterable by: source, compliance status, category, and whether key fields are missing.
The Product Create and Edit form has these fields organized in clear sections:
Basic Info: product name (required), description, category (dropdown from our category tree — required), barcode (EAN-13 format), image URL, and tags (multi-select from: Bestseller, New Arrival, Recommended, Seasonal, High Margin, Fast Mover).
Sourcing Info: source (dropdown: china_haoduobao, china_allen, india_deodap, india_wholesaledock, india_basketo, india_other — required), supplier name, EXW price in Yuan (for China sources), wholesale price in INR (for India sources), minimum order quantity, units per carton, and carton dimensions (length, width, height in cm — needed for freight calculation on China products).
Pricing: this section should be mostly auto-calculated by the price engine. The product team enters the source price, and the system fills in: FOB price, CIF price, customs duty, IGST, total landed cost, our markup (from the global settings), partner price, suggested MRP (rounded to the nearest MRP band: 29, 49, 79, 99, 129, 149, 199, 249, 299, 399, 499), and the partner's margin percentage. The product team can override the suggested MRP if needed. The whole pricing chain should be visible here (this is an internal page — the partner never sees this).
Compliance: compliance status (dropdown: safe, restricted, banned — required), BIS required (yes/no checkbox), compliance notes (free text — e.g., "BIS certification mandatory for toys, do not list"), label status (dropdown: english, chinese, needs_relabel), and a prominent red warning banner if the product is marked as banned explaining it cannot appear in the partner catalog.
Inspired by Marg's Item Master which has 52 fields across tabs — we're at about 25-30 fields which is the right complexity for our business. Every field we track here directly serves a purpose somewhere else in the system: barcodes power the POS, compliance flags filter the partner catalog, pricing feeds the order system, carton dimensions enable freight calculation.
The Categories page should support the 3-level hierarchy we defined: Level 1 is the Zone (like "Kitchen Zone," "Personal Care Zone," "Stationery Zone"), Level 2 is the Category within that zone (like "Storage & Organization," "Cooking Utensils"), Level 3 is the Subcategory (like "Containers," "Racks," "Baskets"). Show this as an expandable tree in the left panel — click a zone to see its categories, click a category to see its subcategories. Each category node shows: name, number of products in it, customs duty percentage, and IGST percentage (these two are important for the price engine). The product team can create, edit, rename, and reorganize categories. They can also drag products between categories if something was miscategorized.
