Pipeline, Lead Management, and Daily WorkflowThis portal is for Harsh, Suprans, and anyone else doing sales. Their entire job is to find people who want to open a value retail store, qualify them, convince them, collect the token payment, and hand them off to the ops team. After that, sales moves on to the next lead. Think of this like a real estate agent's CRM — the pipeline IS their job.When a sales person logs in, their dashboard should be a morning briefing. At the very top, a row of 4 number cards: total leads assigned to me, leads needing followup today (where the next followup date is today or overdue), leads in active negotiation (in the "Proposal Sent" or "Negotiation" stages), and tokens collected this month (count of leads that moved to "Token Paid" this month). These numbers tell the salesperson exactly what their day looks like in 2 seconds.Below the numbers, a "Today's Followups" section — a list of leads where the next followup date is today or past due, sorted by priority (Hot leads first, then Warm, then Nurture). Each lead in this list shows: name, city, their score badge (Hot in red, Warm in yellow, Nurture in green — this scoring already exists in our system), their current pipeline stage, when they were last contacted, and a quick-action "Call" button (opens tel: link) and "WhatsApp" button (opens wa.me link with their number). The sales person works through this list top to bottom every morning.The Pipeline page is the big one — a Kanban board showing all leads organized by stage. We already have 9 stages defined: New Inquiry, Qualification Sent, Discovery Call, Proposal Sent, Negotiation, Token Paid, In Execution, Launched, Lost. Each stage is a column. Each lead is a card in the column. The card shows: person's name, city, score badge (Hot/Warm/Nurture), selected package if any (Lite/Pro/Elite), total investment amount, and days since last contact (shows red if over 7 days — means they're going cold). The sales person should be able to drag a card from one column to the next to advance it through stages. When they drop a card into a new stage, show a quick form asking: what happened (a note), when's the next followup (date picker), and save. This creates an activity entry on that lead's history.The sales person should be able to filter the pipeline: "My Leads" vs "All Leads" (for when we have multiple salespeople), by city, by score (show me only Hot leads), by package, and by stage. There should also be a simple list view toggle for people who prefer tables over Kanban — same data, just in a sortable table format.Clicking a lead card opens a Lead Detail page showing everything about that person. Top section: name, phone, email, city, score breakdown (each of the 6 scoring factors — Budget, Location, Operator, Timeline, Experience, Engagement — with their individual scores), their selected package, and total investment amount. Below that, a tabbed section:Activity tab: a reverse-chronological timeline of every interaction — "March 15: Discovery call completed. Interested in Pro package. Next followup March 18." Each entry shows the date, what happened, and who logged it. The sales person can add new entries from here — just a text field and save.Details tab: all the qualification form data they filled out (if they completed the public qualification form on our website, this data auto-populated into the system with auto-scoring). Plus store details if they've shared: address, area, rent, floor, nearby landmark.Payments tab: shows payment milestones for this lead — has the token been paid? Amount? Date? Mode? This is read-only for sales — ops handles the actual payment tracking.Notes tab: free-form notes the sales person writes about this lead. Separate from the activity timeline which is structured.The sales person should be able to edit the lead's basic info (name, phone, email, city), change their assigned sales person, update the followup date, and add activity entries. They should NOT be able to see or edit: product pricing, cost breakdowns, margin calculations, supplier info, or any internal economics. They see package names and investment ranges, nothing more.Load everything from mock JSON files: mockLeads.json with 15-20 leads spread across different stages with different scores and cities, mockActivities.json with sample activity entries per lead, and mockSalesUsers.json with 2-3 sales team members for the assignment dropdown.
